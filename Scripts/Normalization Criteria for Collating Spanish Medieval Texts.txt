Normalization Criteria for Collating Spanish Medieval Texts

These are suggested criteria for the normalization (not modernization!) of Spanish medieval texts to facilitate their collation.

They are by no means exhaustive—rather, they are continuously revised—and they rely on examples taken from the vocabulary of Sancho IV's
Lucidario, transcribed according to the Hispanic Seminary of Medieval Studies' transcription guidelines, as proposed in David Mackenzie's
"A Manual of Manuscript Transcription for the Dictionary of Old Spanish Language" (1997, 5th revised edition).

The processes described in this document include:

— The regularization of vowels and consonants according to their phonetic value (v ~ u, i ~ j ~ y, and geminate consonants at the
beginning of words).
— The separation of words written in a single stroke but consisting of two independent syntactic units (e.g., article + verb,
pronoun + article). More complex cases include: proclitic pronouns + verbs, verbs + enclitic pronouns, and the clitics and auxiliaries
of analytic futures and conditionals.
— The use of capital letters to distinguish proper nouns.
— The joining of words written in two strokes but forming a single unit

These minor alterations aim to facilitate the process of comparing and contrasting different witnesses of a work using automated collation
software (in this case, ChrysoCollate, available here: https://cental.uclouvain.be/chrysocollate/).

This approach is expands several scripts developed by José Manuel Fradejas Rueda for normalizing transcriptions of the Siete Partidas corpus,
available here: https://github.com/7PartidasDigital/XML-TEI/tree/master/scripts.
